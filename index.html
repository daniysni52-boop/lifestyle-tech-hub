Who Should Use Chime?

Chime is the best option if you want a genuine bank replacement with zero fees. The SpotMe overdraft protection alone saves the average American over $300 per year compared to traditional banks. Who Should Use Cash App?

Cash App wins when your priority is sending money and investing. The ability to buy fractional stocks starting at $1 makes it the go-to for people just starting to invest. The Cash Card Boosts offer real instant cashback at major merchants. Just do not use it as a savings account — the 0% APY is a deal-breaker.

Our Verdict

For everyday banking with no fees and real savings growth, Chime is the clear winner. For sending money and dipping into investing, Cash App is the better tool. Many people use both — Chime as their main account, Cash App for P2P payments and stocks.

5 Signs You Should Switch to a Neobank in 2026

By Daniel Y.  |  July 2026  |  GuideSavings

Still paying monthly fees to your traditional bank? Here are five signs it is time to make the switch.

1. You pay a monthly maintenance fee

If your bank charges you $10-15 per month just to keep your account open, you are paying $120-180 per year for nothing. Every major neobank offers $0 monthly fees.

2. Your savings earn basically nothing

The average big bank savings APY is 0.01%. Neobanks are offering 2-5% right now. On a $5,000 emergency fund, that is the difference between $0.50 and $250 per year.

3. You have been hit with overdraft fees

The average overdraft fee is $35. Chime's SpotMe covers up to $200 in overdrafts with zero fees. One overdraft at a traditional bank costs more than a year of neobanking.

4. You pay ATM fees regularly

Out-of-network ATM fees average $4.73 per transaction. Chime gives you access to 60,000+ free ATMs. If you withdraw cash twice a week, you save nearly $500 per year.

5. You have never needed to walk into a branch

If your last branch visit was to deposit a check — which you can now do with your phone — there is no reason to keep paying traditional bank fees. Mobile check deposit, instant notifications and 24/7 app access make branches irrelevant for most people.
