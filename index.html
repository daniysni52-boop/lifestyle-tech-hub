Cash App: Best for sending money and investing

Cash App is not really a bank. It is more of a financial Swiss Army knife. You can split a bill, buy $5 worth of Apple stock, and pick up some Bitcoin, all from the same app. The Cash Card Boosts give you instant discounts at real stores.

By Daniel Y.   Guide Savings

Most people stick with their bank out of habit. But if any of these sound familiar, you might be losing hundreds of dollars a year without realizing it.

Chase, Bank of America and Wells Fargo all charge $10 to $15 per month unless you meet certain conditions. That is up to $180 per year for nothing. Neobanks charge zero.

The national average savings APY at big banks is 0.01%. Chime pays 2.00%. On a $5,000 emergency fund, that is $250 per year vs $0.50. The math is not close.

One $35 overdraft fee costs more than a year of neobanking. Chime covers up to $200 in overdrafts at no cost through SpotMe. It is one of the most genuinely useful features in any banking app right now.

You pay ATM fees on a regular basis

Out of network ATM fees average almost $5 per transaction. Chime gives you 60,000 plus free ATMs. Two withdrawals per week adds up to nearly $500 saved per year.

You have not been inside a branch in years

If mobile check deposit, online transfers and the app handle everything you need, there is no reason to keep paying traditional bank fees for a service you are not using.
